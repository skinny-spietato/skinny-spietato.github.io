BLACK SHEEP X HEARTLESS X BEKIND X APOLLO

Black Sheep hosted a two-day collaborative event structure on May 1st and 2nd at Apollo Milano, integrating external formats: Bekind from Rome and Heartless from Paris, alongside a secondary backroom intervention by Los Cubanflow. The objective was to produce a coherent series of digital promotional assets capable of presenting the multi-format event with high visual clarity. The project required the adaptation of the primary brand identifier the Black Sheep mascot into a flexible illustrative system capable of incorporating the distinct identity of each guest format while maintaining the core structural recognition of the host brand.

The visual communication required a system capable of supporting different narrative tones: a refined, 70s-inspired presentation for the Bekind event, and a darker, emotionally charged aesthetic for the Heartless event. The challenge was to create cohesive digital flyers and animated assets that clearly communicated the specific daily lineups, dress codes, and atmospheres without breaking the overarching visual language of the Black Sheep community. The assets had to function across digital platforms to effectively promote the consecutive dates.

The production process shifted from the standard 3D rendering pipeline to a specialized 2D workflow. The original 3D mascot was re-engineered into a hand-illustrated cartoon visual model. The static illustrations were then processed and animated utilizing Adobe After Effects to produce dynamic motion assets. This workflow made it possible to exert precise control over character styling, environmental lighting simulation, and contextual accessories, ensuring high visual consistency across the digital deliverables while allowing for format-specific customization.

The visual system was bifurcated to reflect the two distinct event identities. For the Bekind collaboration, the mascot was placed in a cool-toned environment, styled in a 70s-inspired vest and collared shirt, and positioned interacting with a vinyl turntable to reflect the classic, refined music selection. For the Heartless collaboration, the environment shifted to a deep red, atmospheric setting. The character was rendered wearing a custom t-shirt featuring a broken heart motif, establishing a direct visual link to the Parisian brand's identity. Both systems share the same technical animation pipeline, ensuring visual coherence despite the difference in tone.

The final digital deliverables include: Hand-illustrated promotional flyers detailing complex daily lineups, animated motion assets designed for social media distribution, and specific backroom visual variants for the Los Cubanflow intervention. These materials were engineered to function as a unified communication campaign, providing clear informational hierarchy regarding locations, dates, and stylistic guidelines such as the 70s dress code. The project transformed a complex, multi-day event schedule into a structured and easily decodable visual narrative. The resulting 2D animated assets provided the brand with a scalable and highly customized digital campaign, reinforcing the collaborative nature of the events while solidifying the central role of the Black Sheep visual identity.

BLACK SHEEP X 11CLUBROOM

The Black Sheep x 11Clubroom project operates within a nightlife context defined by strong urban and community identity, designed for clubbing environments where the visual experience is central. The primary visual communication element consists of immersive LED walls engineered to deliver spatial continuity and aesthetic coherence, transforming the venue into an integrated and recognizable visual system.

The project objective was to produce a visually neutral and immediately recognizable element, capable of integrating into the underground identity of the brand without referencing specific cultural or realistic imagery. The visual system was developed to reduce cultural stereotypes associated with events categorized as "Afro night," expand cross-cultural accessibility, and ensure instant readability under the low-light conditions typical of nightclub environments.

The core of the system is a stylized 3D mascot with compact proportions and smooth forms, engineered to remain legible and defined on large-format LED walls. The mascot face is rendered with high-contrast graphic design: uniform black surfaces and sharp white details, ensuring readability in fully dark environments. The character carries distinctive accessories — a cap bearing the inscription "(B)LACK(S)HEEP NO (B)ULL(S)HIT" and a white t-shirt with the BS logo — reinforcing the direct connection to the event identity. The overall aesthetic is synthetic and symbolic, functioning as an autonomous and instantly recognizable visual signal.

From a technical standpoint, the mascot is a complete 3D asset comprising modeling, texturing, rigging, animation and final rendering. Three synchronized visual sets were produced for two distinct LED walls in the venue: one primary wall positioned behind the DJ console and one secondary wall near the entrance. Each set contains two synchronized video outputs designed to create visual continuity between the entrance zone and the dance floor. All content is programmed in seamless loop to maintain visual coherence throughout the entire duration of the event.

Within the space, the mascot operates as a recurring and active presence, extending beyond a passive background element. It welcomes, observes, moves and reacts, progressively introducing the audience to the experience while maintaining a constant visual identity. The visual intervention is minimal but strategic — embedded into the real spatial context of the club, it becomes a structural component of the overall visual system. The result is a dynamic installation that combines coherence, legibility and recognizability, elevating the audience experience without dispersing attention from the brand identity.

AEEZUS ANKH — TRUCKER HAT

Aeezus Ankh introduced a limited collection of five hats: one black signature piece and four colorful editions distributed through a mystery box system. The objective was to produce a series of digital assets capable of presenting the products with a high level of visual clarity and realism. The content had to communicate material quality, design details, and stylistic identity while maintaining a consistent visual language across all pieces. The project required the creation of two complementary visual approaches: a refined and minimal presentation for the black hat, and a more dynamic narrative centered around the mystery box concept for the colorful editions.

The brand needed visual content capable of presenting the collection in a way that would clearly communicate the product's design, materials, and identity across digital platforms. The challenge was to create a cohesive visual system that could support two different presentation styles without breaking the overall consistency of the collection. The assets had to function across multiple contexts including social media, advertising campaigns, and e-commerce environments.

The production process combined digital modeling, product photography, and animation in order to construct a flexible set of visual assets. The hats were first recreated as detailed digital garments using CLO 3D, allowing accurate simulation of fabric structure and proportions. The models were then exported and refined inside Blender, where texturing, lighting, and animation were developed to produce realistic motion and presentation. This workflow made it possible to maintain full control over lighting conditions, camera angles, and product movement while ensuring visual consistency across the entire collection.

The visual system was designed to highlight two complementary narratives. The black hat was presented through a minimal and controlled visual environment focused on rotation, lighting, and material definition. The objective was to emphasize craftsmanship, structure, and elegance. The colorful editions were introduced through a mystery box concept where the product is revealed dynamically. This approach creates anticipation while allowing each color variation to emerge as a distinct visual moment. Both systems share the same technical pipeline and lighting logic, ensuring visual coherence despite the difference in tone.

The final assets include: High resolution product photography, Realistic 3D product visualizations, Animated sequences highlighting product form and motion, Close-up images showing stitching, textures, and finishing details. These materials were designed to be easily adaptable for social media, advertising campaigns, and online product presentation. The project produced a versatile visual asset system capable of presenting the collection with clarity and visual impact. By combining photography and physically based 3D rendering, the project delivered realistic representations of the products while allowing flexible storytelling across different platforms. The result is a coherent set of visual materials that enhances product perception and supports the brand's digital communication.

The project transformed a small product collection into a structured visual narrative combining product realism, controlled presentation, and flexible digital assets. The resulting materials provide the brand with a scalable content system suitable for marketing, communication, and e-commerce environments.

FATAL BLOOM BY MANTIS

This project focuses on the development of the mini collection Fatal Bloom, a brand created by Mantis. The work centered on designing an apparel system composed of three garments — T-shirt, tank top and shorts — conceived as elements of a modular structure rather than isolated products. The objective was to build a collection capable of generating multiple configurations through the physical interaction between garments while maintaining a coherent technical framework. The T-shirt features a cropped oversized structure with fourteen metal eyelets placed over the graphic print and two half-zippers running across the back from sleeve to sleeve, allowing the upper section of the tank top to attach and create a layered configuration. The tank top acts as the central connector of the system, integrating vertical and horizontal zippers that allow the garment to split into two sections, enabling the upper part to connect to the T-shirt and the lower part to attach to the shorts. The shorts complete the system with rear half-zippers that extend from the center back toward the front of the leg, allowing the lower section of the tank top to connect and complete the modular configuration. Alongside the garment design two print variations were developed for the T-shirt, while the collection relies on a restricted palette and a selection of materials and metallic hardware elements that reinforce the industrial and grunge visual language of the project. The work concluded with the development of complete technical tech packs for the manufacturer, enabling the collection to move directly into the production phase.

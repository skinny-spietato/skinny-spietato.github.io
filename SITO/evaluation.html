[01. DATA CONTROLLER]
The architect and system administrator of SITOSS. Centralized contact and notifications managed exclusively through the domain's extraction nodes. Contact Email for Privacy Inquiries: INFO.SKINNYSPIETATO@GMAIL.COM

[02. LEGAL BASIS FOR PROCESSING]
The input and processing of information flows within this terminal are subject to the performance of pre-contractual measures (Art. 6.1.b GDPR) and the legitimate interest of the Data Controller (Art. 6.1.f GDPR) in protecting serverless cloud infrastructures from bot vectors or non-commercial intents.

[03. COOKIE ARCHITECTURE AND LOCAL IDENTIFIERS]
The system does not implement commercial profiling cookies or third-party pixels (Meta, Google Analytics). The environment relies solely on isolated first-party technical identifiers:
* Local Storage -> Retention of global operational states (verification, request, verified, allowed, punished).
* Temporary Storage -> Secure retention (600s) of the OTP token tied to the user's email address.
Prior consent via banner is not required as these are strictly necessary vectors for the logical delivery of the interface.

[04. SCOPE OF PROCESSING AND DUAL-TIER PROFILING]
The generated text flows, geographical metadata extracted via geofencing mechanisms, and Base64 binary streams loaded into the Staging Area are analyzed by an integrated backend pipeline and external LLM clusters. Data transferred to external LLM providers outside the EU is protected by Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs).

[05. PUNITIVE LOGIC AND AUTOMATED DECISION-MAKING PROCESSES (ART. 22 GDPR)]
The software module 'The Analyst' performs automated screening by cross-referencing Scope and Budget. Requests not aligned with the minimum parameters (e.g., BUDGET_TOO_LOW) or lacking commercial intent (CV, reverse engineering) result in the immediate and permanent exclusion of the instance, archived in the isolated registry 'SAVE_REJECTED'. Attempting fraudulent interruption of the input flow triggers the 'Punishment Protocol' with temporary coercive blocking of the interface.

[06. RETENTION PERIOD (MINIMIZATION)]
Validated and admitted lead data remains imprinted in the 'REQUESTS' database for a maximum of 24 months for commercial follow-up. Records allocated in the 'SAVE_REJECTED' discard registry are subjected to periodic destructive thermal self-purging protocols every 30 days.

[07. DATA SUBJECT RIGHTS (ART. 15-21 GDPR)]
The user retains the right to request human intervention and review of automated decisions. Furthermore, users have the right to request access, rectification, erasure (right to be forgotten), restriction of processing, and data portability. Requests can be forwarded to the system administrator via the provided privacy email. Users also have the right to lodge a complaint with the National Supervisory Authority.
